Assignment 4

Slouching Tigers, Hiding Dragons

  • In place of the standard MNIST dataset, we will use Google's open-source Quick, Draw! doodle dataset.
  • Our focus will be on two datasets consisting of random quick doodles of dragons and tigers.
  • Use a TensorFlow front-end called Keras to develop a classifier which can distinguish a dragon doodle from a tiger doodle.
    Experiment with various machine learning support provided in this platform.

What to submit

Submit via email (as a compressed tarball) your source code (do not include the datasets) for the homework. Arrange an online demostration of your program. You may work in groups of three for this assignment.

If you use ideas or existing implementations from elsewhere, please acknowledge and document your source.

Bonus

Extend the above problem to include at least 10 classes (different categories of doodles). Design a learning algorithm that returns a classifier which returns a probability distribution over the classes (for example, via the softmax function).
